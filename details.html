RL-based loss function

In Equation (2) and (3) of the main test we have introduced the gradient descent method to update our network parameters `\theta` by computing differential of the RL-base loss: `\nabla_{\theta} L_{RL}(\theta) = - \sum_{S} \nabla_{\theta} r(S)p(S | F; \theta)`

Since the reward `r(S)` is not a function that depends on `\theta`, it is not differentiable with regard to `\theta`, so this gradient can be rewritten as: `\nabla_{\theta} L_{RL}(\theta) = - \sum_{S} r(S) \nabla_{\theta} p(S | F; \theta)\ ` `= - \sum_{S} p(S | F; \theta) r(S) \frac{\nabla_{\theta} p(S|F; \theta)}{p(S | F; \theta)}` `= - \sum_{S} p(S | F; \theta) r(S) \nabla_{\theta} log p(S | F; \theta)\ ` `= - \mathbb{E}_{S~p} [r(S) \nabla_{\theta} log p(S | F; \theta)]`

In Equation (4) of the main text we reduce the RL-based loss with a baseline `b`. When we use `r(S^b)` as the baseline: `\nabla_{\theta} L_{RL}(\theta) = - \mathbb{E}_{S~ p} [(r(S) - r(S^b)) \nabla_{\theta} log p(S | F; \theta)]`

Please note that the baseline can reduce the variance of the gradient without changing the expected value of gradient, since: `\sum_{S} b \nabla_{\theta} p(S | F; \theta) = b \nabla_{\theta} \sum_{S} p(S | F; \theta) = b\nabla_{\theta} 1 = 0`

When Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) is applied, gradient update for each video processed, then this gradient `\nabla_{\theta}` can be approximated by Monte-Carlo (MC) sampling through a single training example: `\nabla_{\theta} L_{RL}(\theta) \approx - (r(S) - r(S^b)) \nabla_{\theta} log p(S | F; \theta)`

The difference between WXE loss and the RL-based loss

WXE is trained on ground truth and the weight in it is calculated on the various target descriptions for one video, while the reward of full RL training is evaluated on sentences sampled from the network.

In WXE, the metric score, as the weight of the loss, represents the quality of each manual annotations; but in RL, the metric score is the reward which evaluates the quality of the generated sentence.

The purpose of introducing weight in WXE is to make the generated sentences closer to those with higher scores among all ground-truth sentences, while the reward of RL is to motivate the model to generate sentences with higher scores.

Because our two-stage method avoids the shortcoming of having to learn an estimate of expected rewards as a baseline in RL-based loss function, it can be more effectively trained on single example of sampled sentences using SGD.
